JULY 21ST 2015

RYDER – EX IDOLO

WRITTEN BY JOE PERKINS

RYDER...

A FRAGRANCE INSPIRED BY THE HISTORY OF MAYFAIR AND ST JAMES

“Matthew Zhuk, creative lead and curator for Ex Idolo fragrances, celebrates both majestic natural forces which come together to bring perfume into our lives; the unhurried process of the natural formation of the precious resins, locked inside living vessels until their discovery, the exacting distillation process and the to the delicate balancing act of blending.” (from the press release)

RYDER WELCOMES THE NOSE WITH AN OPENING SCENE WORTHY OF AN OSCAR.

The refined rustic quality immediately sends my synapses firing; it’s both familiar and unfamiliar simultaneously.

To my nose, Ryder smells like snippets of other respected fragrances which, when combined, creates something that’s profoundly unique.

The first thing to strike me is a warm and rich, cedar-like note that reminds me of pencil shavings drenched in booze. It’s nostalgic and strangely comforting.

There’s an ashy-tobacco scent that takes me back to a night wearing Bull’s Blood by Imaginary Authors. But Ryder, unlike Bull’s Blood, isn’t offensive and actually smells pleasant and classy. Bravo.

It’s not long before a smooth Jasmine note wisps me away to a place I’ve been before with Anubis by Papillon Perfumery. This can only be a good thing.

Notably, Ryder features an ultra-rare Omani (Hougari) frankincense which connects with my spiritual side – it’s calming, relaxing and generally very pleasant. This experience caused my mind to make a knee-jerk connection with Interlude by Amouage (without the obnoxious undertones); it has stark similarities, being boozy, thick on the skin and beautifully blended.

“True to the traditions of the grand houses of the golden age of perfume, Ex Idolo has created an exceptional blend inspired by the history of the members clubs of Mayfair and St. James in London. The fragrance is a juxtaposition of sweet tobacco and boozy notes against a warm woody-amber accord. The fragrance is anchored by a sparkling and ultra rare Omani (Hougari) frankincense. Although inspired by the past, Ryder is a modern fragrance; it is worth mentioning that although many of the members clubs started as male-only – a large number of them now invite women as well. To represent this, Ryder’s distinctive character has been enhanced with a heady, indolic Royal jasmine. Ryder is a unisex fragrance.”(from the press release)

With its roots anchored in an historic era, Ryder is opulent and pretentious in the best possible way.

I love the soft amber note which delicately knits everything together, and the smokey vanilla dry-down (which lasts 24 hours) is only ever a wrist-sniff away – it’s not dissimilar to The Sexiest Scent on the Planet here, which is a massive compliment.

Ryder would suit that formal occasion – as you sit on a mahogany Chesterfield sofa, smoking a Cuban cigar (Cohiba Esplendido) and sipping fine whisky from a crystal tumbler (no ice).

Inspired by the past, Ryder illuminates the present.

Suave.

Some Notes

Frankincense, Jasmine, Amber, Dark Woods, Resins.

Three Words

Rich. Bold. Decadent.

Availability

FENWICK, LES SENTEURS, ROULLIER WHITE, BRUMMELLS (£90.00).

Other Stuff

Matthew Zhuk kindly provided a sample of Ryder on which I based this review.
